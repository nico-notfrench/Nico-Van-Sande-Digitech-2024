2023 Project

The Unfortunate Pirate

Over the course of last year we completed one topic per term for 4 terms,
Image Manipuation, Computer Science, Programming with Scratch and Character
Design. In these 4 courses we brushed over the basics in a few programs
such as Scratch, Inkscape and Gimp. We also learned about the basics of
Binary numbers, algorithms and text compression along with a couple other things.

I thoroughly enjoyed these topics. It being my first year in Digi-tech I
hadn't really had much experience with these topics, but I was able to
learn a lot and I was able to apply my knowledge in the final task,character
design which I am going to showcase here.


For my very last project of the year, because of my love of Lego I chose
to create a Lego Pirate in a multitude of outcomes. It all started with
an idea, an unlucky pirate. Perhaps with no legs or hands, but why not take
it further and get rid of both its eyes as well. Once the idea was set it
needed some sort of character design, so I found some Lego rendering
software (Lego Studio) and began creating (The Lego design are the first
2 images).

Next was the vector art, I used Inkscape, which we learned earlier in the
year, to begin tracing and fine tuning the design. About a week and a lot
of Spotify later I had a design, but it lacked something, dimension, it
looked really flat where as the Lego design had a lot of detail. I was
able to fix this by using shading as shown in the third image.

But why stop there? With only an image? Surely there could be more.
And there was, 3D modelling. I used Blender to recreate my renders
and art to create a printable model.
