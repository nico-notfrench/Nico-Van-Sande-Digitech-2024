Reflection

How do you feel your web development journey has gone? What did you like,
dislike etc. What have you learnt?

My web development journey over the past year has been very interesting. Starting with
the basics and building up to advanced techniques has given me a strong knowledge of web
design and the development of it.

I enjoyed the creativity of designing good looking and functional webpages. Learning how
to bring ideas to life through HTML and CSS was incredibly interesting. Debugging and
problem-solving any challenges was quite rewarding, as they helped me grow both my
understanding and my ability to problem solve quicky.

The things I disliked were the little problems that kept coming up in my code, missing a
">" or a "/". Although it was frustrating at first, once all the debugging and fixing was
done I felt really and happy with how it turned out.

Throughout this year, I have learnt:
HTML and CSS basics: Structuring, styling, and adding interactivity to my website.
Image Creation: Creating and manipulating images to create unique designs.
Computer Science: Hexa decimal, Binary and Sorting. The code that makes a computer run.
Programming: Learning simple Python programmes to recieve user inputs andact upon them.
3D modelling: The ins and outs of fusion.
Animation: How to pose and move animals and people in Blender.



Overall what do you think of your website and how it looks and works.
Why did you style it the way you have?

I’m proud of how my website looks and functions. The design reflects my personal style
and prioritises useability. To achieve a modern look and to unsure a great user experience, I used:

Typography: Clean, readable fonts for accessibility.
Colour scheme: A consistent palette to add colour and contrast the boring text.
Layouts: Making sure each page is layed out in its own way, unique to what it is presenting.
The site is functional and performs well, I'm happy with how this website turned out.



Are you ready to undertake your own project over the next 12 weeks?

Looking back, I have developed a wide range of skills, I feel confident in my ability
to continue withmore difficult, complex projects on my own. These weeks of learning have
equipped me with the tools to begin my own project over the next 12 weeks. I’m ready to
create something unique and interesting, to solve problems, and bring that vision to life.
