Reflection on My Web Development Journey My web development journey over the past year has been very interesting. Starting with the basics and building up to advanced techniques has given me a strong knowledge of web design and the development of it. What I Liked I enjoyed the creativity of designing good looking and functional webpages. Learning how to bring ideas to life through HTML and CSS was incredibly interesting. Debugging and problem-solving any challenges was quite rewarding, as they helped me grow both my understanding and my ability to problem solve quicky. What I Disliked One aspect I found less enjoyable was dealing with cross-browser compatibility issues. It required extra time and effort to ensure the site looked and functioned consistently across various platforms. Managing responsive design intricacies for complex layouts was also challenging but ultimately rewarding. What I Learnt Throughout this year, I have learnt: HTML and CSS basics: Structuring, styling, and adding interactivity to my website. Image Creation: Creating and manipulating images to create unique designs. Computer Science: Hexa decimal, Binary and Sorting. The code that makes a computer run. Programming: Learning simple Python programmes to recieve user inputs andact upon them. 3D modelling: Animation: How to pose and move animals and people in Blender. I’m proud of how my website looks and functions. The design reflects my personal style and emphasises clarity and usability. I prioritised a clean and modern aesthetic, ensuring an intuitive user experience. The styling choices were deliberate: Typography: I used clean, readable fonts for accessibility. Colour scheme: A consistent palette to convey professionalism and cohesion. Layouts: Grid systems ensured structure and visual appeal. The site is functional and performs well across devices, meeting the project goals effectively. Reflection on Learning and Readiness Reflecting on the wide range of skills I’ve developed, I feel confident in my ability to approach complex projects independently. From front-end to back-end concepts, I’ve built a robust foundation that I’m eager to apply. The 26 weeks of learning have equipped me with the tools to undertake my own project over the next 12 weeks. I’m ready to set clear goals, solve problems creatively, and bring a unique vision to life.
